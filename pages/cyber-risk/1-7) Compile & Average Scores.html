Compile and Average the Scores
4
After the interviews are over, find the average score for each question and put the results into the "AVG. Response" column, starting with cell F18, below. Calculate the average by (1) adding together the scores from all interviewees for each question. Then (2) divide the result by the total number of interviewees. The results will automatically carry over to tab 1-8, where you will identify your top cyber risks.

For example, if Bill gave a score of 3 for question ID.AM-1, Jane gave a score of 5, and Sally said 4, put the average of 4 into cell F19. Here is the math:

AVG Response = (Bill’s Score + Jane’s Score + Sally's score)/number of interviewees
AVG Response = (3 + 5 + 4)/3
AVG Response = 12/3
AVG Response = 4
5
You could also calculate the averages by using Google Sheets functions. We can’t do this ahead of time because we don’t know how many interviews you’ve done. The following example is what you would put into cell F19:

=AVERAGE('Interview_Bill'!F8,'Interview_Jane'!F8,'Interview_Sally'!F8)

Where 'Interview_Bill' is the name of the interview sheet and 'F8' is the cell on that interview sheet that contains Bill's score for question ID.AM-1.

The averages for all five functions are automatically calculated for you in the table starting at cell F10.
